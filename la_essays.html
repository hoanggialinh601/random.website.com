Argumentative Essay Writing - The Basics 🧐

ⓘ An essay has 3 main components: the intro, the body and the conclusion.

🔖 The introduction includes:

🔖 Hook: grabs a reader's attention. A hook can take on many forms:

◦ Reference to actual event

◦ Interesting fact/ statistic/ trend/ myth (that is later debunked)

◦ A work of fiction or popular culture most people would be familiar with

◦ Anecdote or story (personal experience) → concise

◦ A provocative question or statement (thought-provoking)

◦ A quotation (from well-known individual) or a well-known quotation

◦ Rhetorical question

◦ Humour

🔖 Background: helps readers understand issues, starting by defining broader context:

◦ Exploring relevant current events, globally or locally

◦ Defining key terms in context to ensure clarity

⚠ Important!

🔖 Foreshadowing: briefly discuss what is going to be covered in the essay, i.e. summary of points:

◦ Why: main ideas

◦ Instead of using vague phrases, e.g. "some might say ...", specify particular stakeholders in opposition to each other

◦ For questions with points of contention ("only", "never", etc.), identify the extreme viewpoint and contrast with another stakeholder who has a more balanced stance.

🔖 Stand

◦ Make stand by incorporating key words in question

◦ Aim for balance by considering opposite viewpoints and explaining why → explaining stance



🔖 The body consists of 1 (or more) POA, 1 (or more) POB and an evaluative rebuttal for the POB.

⚠ When writing a body paragraph, use relevant explanations that provide logical flow to the stand and main idea. Avoid repetitive, unnecessary, run-on (clunky connection of 2 or more ideas) and overly lengthy sentences, in addition to expression and WW/ WoW (wrong word) errors. When developing a point and relevant explanation, do not base the explanation solely on an example (example-driven). Remember to use connectors/ discourse markers, persuasive language and many descriptive adjectives and adverbs.

• Point:

◦ Topic sentence should include a main idea/ reason and a main stand. Both should be made clear.

◦ The point should be detailed, with no repeating ideas

◦ The point should be answer the question using persuasive techniques

◦ 💡 Sample framework: ISMELLU

I Does it uphold an idea?
S Is it sensible?
M Is it a moral thing to do?
E Is it ethical?
L Is it legal?
L Is it logical?
U Is it utilitarian?

• Explanation:

◦ How: elaboration of explanation, specific details

◦ Why: clear reasons for topic sentence

⟹ Comparisons, unique characteristics (details)

◦ When writing explanation, expand to different perspectives: the government, citizens, seniors, children, other dependents, corporations, NGOs, youths, netizens, schools, etc.

⚠ The rebuttal must be aligned to the POB. It is not a contradiction of POB, nor is it another POA to "oppose" the POB. Rather, it is meant to prove that the POB is not as valid a point. When writing a rebuttal, you should follow the framework:

◦ Time: long term versus short term

◦ Impact: real change and impact (Is the impact limited?)

◦ Perception: the ideal versus reality

◦ People: the majority versus the minority

◦ Status quo: now versus later

• Example:

◦ Should not be mere statements of facts but should be used to support the topic sentence and convince the reader to take the main stand.

◦ More specific details to expand the explanation

• Link: link example to topic sentence: how the example emphasises the original point (nuanced)



🔖 The conclusion summarises the main stand and points (i.e. foreshadowing), touches on outcomes facing forward and alludes back to introduction (hook).
